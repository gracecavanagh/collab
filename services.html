Data Acquisition and Preprocessing

Data Acquisition is done by using LMX 200 GPR (Ground Penetration Radar). This device allowed us ti gather underground utility and identify the diameter and material of the utility of the infrastructure (Sensors & Software Inc., n.d., sec. overview). The data collection takes place in Emily Provicial park on April 31st and Mat 1st, 2024. Thhe large area of the park posed a challenge for data collection of the whole park in a single day. TO address this, we opted to concentrate on an area of higher underground utility infrastructure, identified through a maintenance map.

The data obtained from Groud Penetration Radar is processed using trial version of EKKO_Project Siftware. TO familiarize the software some tutorial webinars provided by the software itself, which comprehend us on the functionality of the software and reflectance of different materials aiding on maaterial identification (Sensors & Software Inc., n.d.-a, sec. webinar). In summary differnet material reflects differnt energy, which is directly proportional to the size of hyperbolas generated by the software is dependent on the diameter of the pipe.

Database Management

We utilized the Infrastructure File Geodatabase from Ontario Parks to create three distinct File Geodatabases using FME (Spatial Database Manipulation Engine).

  • Emily Park: The Emily Park Geodatabase encompasses the entire park, integrating all amenities and utility data.
  • Comfort Station: In the Study Area Geodatabase, we focused exclusively on comfort stations, augmenting data with attributes like diameter and pipe materials.
  • Flood Simulation Analysis: Flood Simulation Analysis Geodatabase, our emphasis was on aligning data categories with client analysis requirements.

Our data processing included comprehensive cleanup and field renaming to ensure clarity and consistency. Domains such as Campsite, SepticTank, PipeDiameter, and PipeMaterial were implemented to standardize data entries, while subtypes organized comfort station features based on specific attributes and functionalities, ensuring efficient data management and integrity maintenance.

Spatial Analysis

We leverage the advanced GIS workflow to obtain the building footprint within the Emily Provincial Park. The primary objectives of using the Lidar data is to enhance the precision and utility of the data. Using the advanced flood simulation tool in ArcGIS version 3.3 we performed flood analysis keeping in mind the issues that campsites with electrical connections may have experienced due to safety concers. Our simulation aimed to demonstrate how this tool's ability to delineate flood risk zones and how the park would fare in differing weathr conditions could be an assest for park's emergency planning an dprovide a resuable framework for employees to generate actionable insights.

Multicriteria decision Analysis is conducted to analyze the risk area within the park. The risk assessment study conducted within Emily Provincial Park integrates several crucial factors aimed at ensuring the safety and accessibility of park visitors. These factors include proximity to water and park office and emergency meeting point, areas of high crowd density, terrain slope, and dense vegetation coverage. Utilizing the Weighted Overlay tool, we developed a Weighted Suitability Surface as the foundation for assessing these criteria.

Visualisation

3D visualization of the park is one of our key deliverable of the project. The generated 3D model serves a useful tool for presentation and planning process. While planning for this project, the client pursuit was to engage the Ontario Park management, and visitors with visual appealing 3D model of the park and park maintenance staff with the compelling 3D visualization of the park with underground utilities.
